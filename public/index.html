How Kaspa Notary Works

A traditional notary witnesses an agreement between two parties. Kaspa Notary does the same thing — except the witness is a blockchain that can never lose records, alter them, or be bribed.

1

Upload & Sign

Upload a PDF of your agreement. Type your name to sign, and pay a small notary fee. Your document is hashed — a unique digital fingerprint — and recorded.

2

Counterparty Signs

Send an invite link to the other party. They review the document, connect any Kaspa wallet, and sign. No account needed — just a wallet.

3

Sealed on the Blockchain

Once both parties sign, a seal transaction is permanently recorded on the Kaspa network. The document's fingerprint, both wallet addresses, and the timestamp are embedded in the transaction — immutable proof that both parties agreed to this exact document at this exact time.

What makes this different?

Cannot be backdated

The Kaspa blockchain timestamps every transaction to the second. No one can claim a document was signed earlier or later than it was.

Cannot be altered

The document's cryptographic hash is sealed on-chain. Change even one pixel of the PDF and the hash won't match — tampering is immediately detectable.

Independently verifiable

Anyone can check the seal transaction on the public Kaspa blockchain. No need to trust us — verify it yourself.

Works with any wallet

KasWare, Kastle, Keystone, or any Kaspa wallet. No special account or software required.

Use cases

Freelance contracts, rental agreements, intellectual property timestamps, partnership agreements, proof of concept filings, research paper priority — anything where you need provable, timestamped agreement between two parties.
